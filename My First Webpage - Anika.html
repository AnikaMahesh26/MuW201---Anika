Music Therapy

What is Music Therapy?

Music therapy uses music in clinical situations to help achieve a range of goals. These could include reducing stress, improving mood, and enhancing self-expression. It can include singing, listening, playing an instrument, or composing music.





The therapeutic effects of music can be used to treat a variety of ailments such as heart disease, depression, autism, substance abuse, Alzheimer's disease, etc. It aids memory, lowers blood pressure, enhances self-esteem, reduces stress, and promotes coping skills. As a whole, it enhances a person's psychological, emotional, physical, cognitive, and social well-being.More specifically, music therapy offers its participants numerous benefits, including improved memory, lowered blood pressure, enhanced communication and social skills through exposure to music with others, and self-reflection (observing emotions, thoughts),Reduce muscle tension, increase self-regulation (develop skills to control your thoughts and emotions), increase motivation, manage pain, and have greater joy.
